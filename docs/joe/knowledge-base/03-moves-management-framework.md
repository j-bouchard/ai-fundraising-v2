# Moves Management Framework

## The Standard 7-Stage Donor Cultivation Model

### Overview of Moves Management Philosophy

Moves management is a disciplined process of developing relationships with donors through a series of planned, strategic interactions ("moves") designed to engage their interest and involvement, ultimately leading to solicitation and stewardship. Each move should have a purpose, advance the relationship, and provide insights that inform the next step.

### Stage 1: Identification (0-2 weeks)

**Purpose:** Determine if prospect meets basic criteria for cultivation

**Entry Criteria:**
- Wealth screening indicates capacity of 5x current giving
- Demonstrates philanthropic behavior
- Has linkage to organization
- Aligns with organizational priorities

**Key Activities:**
- Wealth screening analysis
- Peer review and rating
- Social media research
- Database record creation
- Initial capacity rating assignment

**Required Data Points:**
- Estimated net worth
- Liquid assets availability
- Real estate holdings
- Business ownership
- Philanthropic history
- Professional background
- Family/personal connections

**Exit Criteria to Stage 2:**
- Capacity confirmed at target level
- At least one linkage identified
- No disqualifying factors found
- Assignment to gift officer made

**Timeline:** Complete within 2 weeks of identification

### Stage 2: Qualification (2-6 weeks)

**Purpose:** Validate capacity and inclination, establish initial contact

**Entry Criteria:**
- Passed identification screening
- Assigned to portfolio
- Contact information verified
- Research packet completed

**Key Activities:**
- Initial outreach (call/email/letter)
- Discovery meeting scheduling
- Peer intelligence gathering
- Interest area identification
- Communication preference documentation

**Qualifying Questions to Answer:**
- Will they take a meeting?
- Do they have capacity we estimated?
- Are they philanthropically inclined?
- Is there interest in our mission?
- What's their giving timeline?

**Exit Criteria to Stage 3:**
- First meeting completed
- Capacity validated within 25%
- Interest in organization confirmed
- No red flags identified
- Next steps agreed upon

**Exit to Disqualified:**
- Declined multiple meeting requests
- No capacity found
- Not charitably inclined
- Explicitly not interested

**Timeline:** Maximum 6 weeks in stage

### Stage 3: Discovery (6-12 weeks)

**Purpose:** Understand motivations, interests, and giving preferences

**Entry Criteria:**
- Completed qualifying meeting
- Demonstrated interest
- Agreed to continued engagement
- Capacity still viable

**Key Activities:**
- 2-3 discovery meetings
- Site visit or tour
- Program deep dives
- Family member engagement
- Volunteer exploration
- Event attendance

**Critical Information to Gather:**
- Philanthropic priorities
- Decision-making process
- Family involvement level
- Recognition preferences
- Planned giving interest
- Other charitable commitments
- Professional/personal goals
- Preferred communication style

**Discovery Meeting Framework:**
1. Personal update (25%)
2. Organizational update (25%)
3. Discovery questions (40%)
4. Next steps (10%)

**Exit Criteria to Stage 4:**
- Philanthropic priorities documented
- Giving capacity refined
- Interest areas mapped to programs
- Relationship depth achieved
- Engagement timeline understood

**Timeline:** 6-12 weeks typical

### Stage 4: Strategy Development (2-4 weeks)

**Purpose:** Create tailored cultivation plan based on discovery

**Entry Criteria:**
- Discovery phase complete
- Clear interest demonstrated
- Capacity confirmed
- Internal support secured

**Key Activities:**
- Internal strategy session
- Proposal concept development
- Ask amount determination
- Solicitor assignment
- Timeline creation
- Materials preparation

**Strategy Components:**
- Target ask amount
- Project/program match
- Recognition package
- Solicitation team
- Timeline to ask
- Contingency plans

**Stakeholder Involvement:**
- Gift officer (lead)
- Program staff
- Leadership/CEO
- Board member (if applicable)
- Finance team

**Exit Criteria to Stage 5:**
- Written strategy approved
- Ask amount set
- Solicitation team confirmed
- Materials prepared
- Timeline agreed

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks maximum

### Stage 5: Cultivation (3-9 months)

**Purpose:** Deepen engagement and build toward solicitation readiness

**Entry Criteria:**
- Strategy approved
- Prospect engaged
- Ask amount determined
- Team assigned

**Cultivation Activity Menu:**

**Education Activities:**
- Program briefings
- Impact presentations
- Beneficiary meetings
- Research updates
- Behind-the-scenes tours

**Engagement Activities:**
- Advisory committee participation
- Volunteer opportunities
- Peer donor introductions
- Board member meetings
- Special event invitations

**Relationship Activities:**
- Personal visits (monthly)
- Phone check-ins
- Handwritten notes
- Birthday/holiday acknowledgments
- Family engagement

**Cultivation Intensity Levels:**

**Light Touch (Monthly):**
- 1 meaningful contact
- 2-3 soft touches
- Quarterly face-to-face

**Medium Touch (Bi-weekly):**
- 2 meaningful contacts
- 4-6 soft touches
- Monthly face-to-face

**High Touch (Weekly):**
- 4 meaningful contacts
- 8-10 soft touches
- Bi-weekly face-to-face

**Exit Criteria to Stage 6:**
- Readiness signals observed
- Ask amount socialized
- Proposal reviewed positively
- Meeting scheduled
- Solicitor prepared

**Readiness Signals:**
- Asks "how can I help?"
- Increases engagement frequency
- Brings family/advisors
- Requests specific information
- References giving plans

**Timeline:** 3-9 months typical, 12 months maximum

### Stage 6: Solicitation (2-4 weeks)

**Purpose:** Present proposal and secure commitment

**Entry Criteria:**
- Cultivation complete
- Readiness confirmed
- Proposal finalized
- Team prepared
- Meeting scheduled

**Pre-Solicitation Checklist:**
- [ ] Proposal document ready
- [ ] Ask amount confirmed
- [ ] Recognition outlined
- [ ] Payment options prepared
- [ ] Team roles defined
- [ ] Backup dates available
- [ ] Follow-up plan ready

**Solicitation Meeting Agenda:**
1. Opening and rapport (10%)
2. Vision and impact (30%)
3. Specific proposal (20%)
4. The ask (5%)
5. Silence and listening (20%)
6. Discussion (10%)
7. Next steps (5%)

**Post-Meeting Actions:**
- Same day: Team debrief
- 24 hours: Thank you message
- 48 hours: Follow-up materials
- 1 week: Next meeting scheduled

**Possible Outcomes:**

**Yes - Move to Stage 7:**
- Commitment made
- Amount agreed
- Timeline set
- Recognition confirmed

**Maybe - Stay in Stage 6:**
- Needs time to consider
- Requires more information
- Wants to involve others
- Negotiating terms

**No - Return to Stage 5 or 7:**
- Not right time
- Amount too high
- Wrong project
- Other priorities

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks to decision

### Stage 7: Stewardship (Ongoing)

**Purpose:** Fulfill promises, recognize giving, and prepare for next gift

**Entry Criteria:**
- Gift committed or received
- Agreement documented
- Recognition preferences known
- Impact timeline established

**Immediate Stewardship (48 hours):**
- CEO thank you call
- Written acknowledgment
- Tax receipt issued
- Internal announcement
- Database updated

**30-Day Stewardship:**
- Gift agreement executed
- Recognition implemented
- Welcome packet sent
- Impact timeline shared
- Team introductions made

**Ongoing Stewardship Calendar:**

**Monthly:**
- Impact updates
- Program newsletters
- Social media engagement

**Quarterly:**
- Financial reports
- Program briefings
- Personal check-ins
- Event invitations

**Annually:**
- Impact report
- Renewal discussion
- Recognition event
- Tax documentation
- Upgrade exploration

**Re-Entry Criteria:**
- Return to Stage 3 for upgrade
- Return to Stage 5 for new project
- Maintain in Stage 7 if not ready

**Timeline:** Minimum 18 months before next solicitation

## Stage Advancement Criteria

### Advancement Decision Framework

**Move Forward When:**
- All exit criteria met
- Prospect responsive
- Momentum building
- Resources available
- Timeline on track

**Stay in Stage When:**
- More information needed
- Relationship building required
- Timing not optimal
- Competition for attention
- Internal resources limited

**Move Backward When:**
- Information proves incorrect
- Circumstances change
- Relationship deteriorates
- Priorities shift
- Red flags emerge

### Stage Duration Guidelines

| Stage | Minimum | Target | Maximum | Action if Exceeded |
|-------|---------|--------|---------|-------------------|
| Identification | 1 week | 2 weeks | 4 weeks | Disqualify or assign |
| Qualification | 2 weeks | 4 weeks | 6 weeks | Disqualify or advance |
| Discovery | 4 weeks | 8 weeks | 12 weeks | Advance or recycle |
| Strategy | 1 week | 2 weeks | 4 weeks | Escalate to manager |
| Cultivation | 3 months | 6 months | 12 months | Solicit or recycle |
| Solicitation | 1 week | 2 weeks | 4 weeks | Decide or withdraw |
| Stewardship | Ongoing | Ongoing | Ongoing | Never ends |

## Portfolio Management Best Practices

### Portfolio Size Guidelines

**Major Gift Officer (Full-time):**
- Qualified prospects: 100-150
- Discovery/Cultivation: 50-75
- Solicitation/Stewardship: 25-50
- Total managed: 125-150

**Principal Gift Officer (Full-time):**
- Qualified prospects: 50-75
- Discovery/Cultivation: 25-40
- Solicitation/Stewardship: 20-30
- Total managed: 75-100

**CEO/Executive Director:**
- Top prospects only: 10-20
- Board-connected: 10-15
- Stewardship only: 10-20
- Total managed: 25-35

### Portfolio Scoring Matrix

**A-Priority (25% of portfolio):**
- Capacity: $100K+
- Readiness: 12 months
- Engagement: High
- Action: Weekly contact

**B-Priority (50% of portfolio):**
- Capacity: $25K-$99K
- Readiness: 12-24 months
- Engagement: Medium
- Action: Monthly contact

**C-Priority (25% of portfolio):**
- Capacity: $10K-$24K
- Readiness: 24+ months
- Engagement: Low
- Action: Quarterly contact

### Time Allocation Framework

**Weekly Time Distribution (40 hours):**
- Direct donor contact: 20 hours (50%)
- Preparation and follow-up: 8 hours (20%)
- Internal meetings: 4 hours (10%)
- Administrative tasks: 4 hours (10%)
- Professional development: 4 hours (10%)

**Daily Structure Template:**
- 9-11am: Donor meetings/calls
- 11am-12pm: Follow-up tasks
- 12-1pm: Lunch (often with donors)
- 1-3pm: Donor meetings/visits
- 3-4pm: Contact reports
- 4-5pm: Next day preparation

## Moves Management Metrics

### Activity Standards and Benchmarks

**Monthly Minimums per Officer:**
- Face-to-face meetings: 12-15
- Qualification meetings: 3-5
- Discovery meetings: 5-8
- Solicitations: 2-3
- Stewardship visits: 5-8

**Annual Performance Targets:**
- Dollars raised: $2-5M
- Number of gifts: 30-50
- New prospects qualified: 30-40
- Proposals delivered: 20-30
- Success rate: 65-75%

### Pipeline Health Indicators

**Healthy Pipeline Characteristics:**
- 3-5x annual goal in pipeline
- Balanced stage distribution
- Consistent movement between stages
- Multiple solicitors involved
- Diverse project designations

**Warning Signs:**
- Stagnant prospects (>6 months same stage)
- Bunched distribution (all in one stage)
- Low qualification rate (<50%)
- High disqualification rate (>30%)
- Single solicitor dependency

### ROI Calculations

**Cost per Dollar Raised:**
```
(Salary + Benefits + Expenses) ÷ Total Raised = CPDR
Target: $0.15-$0.20 per dollar
```

**Return on Investment:**
```
(Total Raised - Total Costs) ÷ Total Costs = ROI
Target: 400-500% return
```

**Efficiency Ratio:**
```
Number of Gifts ÷ Number of Asks = Success Rate
Target: 65-75% success
```

## Decision Trees for Next Moves

### Post-Meeting Decision Tree

```
Meeting Completed
├── Went Well
│   ├── Ready for Ask → Schedule Solicitation
│   ├── Nearly Ready → Plan Final Cultivation
│   └── Building Interest → Continue Cultivation
├── Neutral Response
│   ├── Need More Info → Provide Materials
│   ├── Timing Issue → Revisit Timeline
│   └── Unclear Interest → Discovery Meeting
└── Went Poorly
    ├── Wrong Project → Pivot Strategy
    ├── Capacity Issue → Adjust Ask Amount
    └── No Interest → Move to Stewardship Only
```

### Stalled Prospect Decision Tree

```
No Movement 60+ Days
├── Responsive to Outreach
│   ├── Scheduling Conflicts → Offer Flexible Options
│   ├── Needs Information → Provide Specific Content
│   └── Waiting on Something → Set Follow-up Date
├── Partially Responsive
│   ├── Sporadic Reply → Change Contact Method
│   ├── Delegates to Others → Engage Full Team
│   └── Always Busy → Reduce Contact Frequency
└── Non-Responsive
    ├── Changed Circumstances → Research and Verify
    ├── Lost Interest → Pause and Recycle
    └── Never Interested → Disqualify
```

### Ask Amount Decision Tree

```
Determining Ask Amount
├── Previous Giving History
│   ├── Consistent Giver → 2-3x Largest Gift
│   ├── Increasing Giver → 3-5x Last Gift
│   └── Major Donor → 1.5-2x Largest
├── No Giving History
│   ├── Wealth Verified → 1% Net Worth
│   ├── Peer Comparison → Match Similar Donors
│   └── Unknown Capacity → Start Conservative
└── Special Circumstances
    ├── Campaign Mode → Stretch Ask (5x)
    ├── Emergency Need → Pragmatic Ask (2x)
    └── Planned Gift → Estate Percentage
```

## Implementation Roadmap

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-2)
- Define stages for organization
- Set advancement criteria
- Create tracking templates
- Train team on methodology
- Pilot with 10 prospects

### Phase 2: Rollout (Months 3-4)
- Migrate existing prospects to stages
- Implement weekly reviews
- Establish reporting rhythms
- Refine stage definitions
- Scale to full portfolio

### Phase 3: Optimization (Months 5-6)
- Analyze conversion rates
- Adjust time-in-stage limits
- Enhance automation
- Develop best practices
- Measure ROI impact

### Phase 4: Maturity (Months 7-12)
- Predictive modeling
- Advanced analytics
- Peer benchmarking
- Continuous improvement
- Scale across organization

This comprehensive framework provides the structure, tools, and strategies needed to implement a successful moves management program that systematically advances donor relationships toward philanthropic partnerships.
