# Gift Officer Daily Workflows Guide

## Daily Task Priorities Framework

### The Gift Officer's Daily Hierarchy

**Priority 1: Revenue-Generating Activities (50% of day)**
- Face-to-face donor meetings
- Solicitation conversations
- Qualification calls
- Cultivation visits
- Stewardship meetings

**Priority 2: Relationship Building (25% of day)**
- Thank you calls
- Email correspondence
- Handwritten notes
- Social touches
- Event follow-ups

**Priority 3: Administrative Excellence (15% of day)**
- Contact reports
- CRM updates
- Proposal development
- Meeting preparation
- Pipeline management

**Priority 4: Strategic Planning (10% of day)**
- Portfolio review
- Move planning
- Strategy development
- Team collaboration
- Professional development

### Daily Non-Negotiables

**The Sacred Seven Daily Tasks:**

1. **Morning Portfolio Review (15 minutes)**
   - Check overnight donor activity
   - Review today's priorities
   - Scan for urgent items
   - Update task list

2. **Prime Time Donor Outreach (9-11am)**
   - Make high-priority calls
   - Schedule meetings
   - Send personalized emails

3. **CRM Updates (30 minutes)**
   - Log all interactions
   - Update contact reports
   - Advance pipeline stages

4. **Afternoon Donor Engagement (2-4pm)**
   - Conduct meetings
   - Make thank you calls
   - Send follow-ups

5. **Written Correspondence (30 minutes)**
   - Draft proposals
   - Write thank you notes
   - Prepare meeting materials

6. **Next Day Planning (15 minutes)**
   - Review tomorrow's calendar
   - Prepare materials
   - Set priorities

7. **Professional Reading (15 minutes)**
   - Industry news
   - Donor research
   - Best practices

## Time Allocation Recommendations

### Optimal Time Distribution Model

**Weekly 40-Hour Breakdown:**

```
External Activities (60% = 24 hours):
- Donor meetings/visits: 12 hours (30%)
- Phone/video calls: 6 hours (15%)
- Events and cultivation: 4 hours (10%)
- Travel time: 2 hours (5%)

Internal Activities (40% = 16 hours):
- Administration/CRM: 6 hours (15%)
- Planning/strategy: 4 hours (10%)
- Team meetings: 3 hours (7.5%)
- Professional development: 3 hours (7.5%)
```

## Portfolio Management Routines

### Daily Portfolio Touch System

**The 5-3-1 Rule:**
- Review 5 top prospects daily
- Make 3 meaningful contacts
- Advance 1 relationship stage

### Weekly Portfolio Review Process

**Monday Morning Protocol (1 hour):**

**Step 1: Pipeline Analysis (20 minutes)**
```
Review by Stage:
- Identification: Who needs qualifying?
- Qualification: Who's ready for discovery?
- Discovery: Who needs strategy?
- Cultivation: Who's ready for ask?
- Solicitation: Who needs follow-up?
- Stewardship: Who needs attention?
```

**Step 2: Priority Setting (20 minutes)**
```
Top 10 Prospects This Week:
1. [Name] - Action needed
2. [Name] - Action needed
3. [Name] - Action needed
(Continue to 10)
```

**Step 3: Calendar Coordination (20 minutes)**
```
Schedule Requirements:
- Meetings needed: X
- Calls required: Y
- Events to attend: Z
- Travel planning: Days
```

### Monthly Portfolio Deep Dive

**First Monday of Month (2 hours):**

**Portfolio Health Check:**
```
Metrics to Calculate:
- Average days in stage
- Conversion rates by stage
- Pipeline value vs. goal
- Activity levels per prospect
- ROI by segment

Red Flags to Address:
- Stalled prospects (60+ days)
- Declining engagement
- Overdue touches
- Missing information
- Unbalanced pipeline
```

**Quarterly Portfolio Rebalancing:**
- Remove unresponsive prospects
- Add new qualified prospects
- Adjust priority rankings
- Update capacity ratings
- Refine strategies

## Best Practices for Success

### Morning Routine Excellence

**The First 30 Minutes:**
1. Review calendar and priorities
2. Check overnight donor emails
3. Scan news for donor mentions
4. Review portfolio dashboards
5. Set daily intentions
6. Prepare materials for meetings

### Meeting Preparation Checklist

**Pre-Meeting (Day Before):**
- [ ] Review donor history
- [ ] Prepare materials
- [ ] Confirm meeting
- [ ] Plan travel route
- [ ] Set meeting objectives
- [ ] Practice ask if applicable

**Day of Meeting:**
- [ ] Review notes
- [ ] Confirm materials packed
- [ ] Check appearance
- [ ] Arrive 15 minutes early
- [ ] Turn off phone
- [ ] Bring business cards

**Post-Meeting (Within 24 hours):**
- [ ] Send thank you
- [ ] Log contact report
- [ ] Schedule follow-ups
- [ ] Share updates with team
- [ ] Plan next moves

## Stress Management and Wellness

### Maintaining Work-Life Balance

**Boundaries to Maintain:**
- No donor calls after 8pm
- One device-free meal daily
- Weekend email limits
- Vacation truly offline
- Lunch breaks protected

**Stress Reduction Techniques:**
- 5-minute meditation breaks
- Walking meetings when possible
- Deep breathing before big asks
- Gratitude journaling
- Peer support groups

**Preventing Burnout:**
- Take all PTO days
- Delegate when possible
- Say no to non-priorities
- Maintain outside interests
- Seek support when needed
